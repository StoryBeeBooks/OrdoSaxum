Accessibility Policy

OrdoSaxum is fundamentally committed to fostering an inclusive, diverse, and accessible environment. We believe in creating a vibrant culture where the unique skills, talents, and experiences of every individual are valued. This diversity is a cornerstone of our innovative approach and enables us to better serve our broad base of clients and partners. Our dedication to continuous improvement extends to ensuring that our services and workplace are accessible to all, including persons with disabilities.

Commitment to Accessibility

OrdoSaxum is steadfast in its commitment to meeting its obligations under the Accessibility for Ontarians with Disabilities Act, 2005 (AODA). We are dedicated to the principles of dignity, independence, integration, and equal opportunity, and we will work diligently to identify, remove, and prevent barriers to accessibility for our customers, employees, and all stakeholders who interact with our business.

Multi-Year Accessibility Plan

To formalize this commitment, OrdoSaxum has developed a Multi-Year Accessibility Plan that outlines our strategy to meet and exceed the requirements of the AODA. This plan is a living document, subject to regular review and updates, demonstrating our proactive steps towards making our organization and the province of Ontario fully accessible. This plan guides our initiatives in technology, process improvement, and training to ensure accessibility.

Accessible Customer Service

OrdoSaxum will make every reasonable effort to ensure that its policies, practices, and procedures are consistent with the principles of the AODA. We are committed to providing goods and services in a way that respects the dignity and independence of people with disabilities.

Communication: We will train our staff to communicate with people with disabilities in ways that take into account their disability.

Assistive Devices: We are committed to serving people with disabilities who use assistive devices to obtain, use, or benefit from our goods and services.

Service Animals and Support Persons: We welcome people with disabilities who are accompanied by a service animal or a support person on the parts of our premises that are open to the public and other third parties.

Leadership and Feedback

The leadership of OrdoSaxum is committed to ensuring our products and services are accessible to everyone. We welcome and value the collaboration and feedback of customers, visitors, and staff so that we may continually improve our accessibility practices. Your input is crucial to our growth as an inclusive organization.

Customers and visitors are encouraged to provide feedback on how we can improve our accessibility. Feedback may be provided through any of the following methods:

  • In person at any of our locations
  • By telephone
  • In writing
  • By email

We will ensure that our feedback process is accessible to people with disabilities by providing or arranging for accessible formats and communication supports, on request. All feedback will be directed to the appropriate personnel and will be addressed promptly.

What Are Cookies?

A "cookie" is a string of information which assigns you a unique identifier that we store on your computer. Your browser then provides that unique identifier to use each time you submit a query to the Site. We use cookies on the Site to, among other things, record your user preferences, track the pages you visit, and understand how the Site is being used.

Types of Cookies

The following types of cookies may be used when you visit the Site:

Necessary Cookies: These cookies are essential for the Site to function properly and to provide certain features and functionalities. Without these cookies, some services on our Website cannot be provided. These are our "first-party cookies" and can be either permanent or temporary. While some of these may be manually disabled in your browser, doing so may affect the functionality of the Site.

Analytics Cookies: These cookies monitor how users reached the Site, and how they interact with and move around once on the Site. The information gathered is used to compile statistical reports on user activity. These cookies let us know what features on the Site are working best and what features can be improved, helping us to enhance the user experience.

Advertising Cookies: These cookies may be placed on your computer by third-party advertisers and ad servers in order to display advertisements that are most likely to be of interest to you. These cookies allow advertisers and ad servers to gather information about your visits to the Site and other websites, alternate the ads sent to a specific computer, and track how often an ad has been viewed and by whom. These cookies are linked to a computer and do not gather any personal information about you.

Controlling Cookies

Most browsers are set to accept cookies by default. However, you can remove or reject cookies in your browser's settings. Please be aware that such action could affect the availability and functionality of the Site. For more information on how to control cookies, check your browser or device's settings for how you can control or reject cookies.

Other Tracking Technologies

In addition to cookies, we may use web beacons, pixel tags, and other tracking technologies on the Site to help customize the Site and improve your experience. A "web beacon" or "pixel tag" is a tiny object or image embedded in a web page or email. They are used to track the number of users who have visited particular pages and viewed emails, and acquire other statistical data. They collect only a limited set of data, such as a cookie number, time and date of page or email view, and a description of the page or email on which they reside. Web beacons and pixel tags cannot be declined.
